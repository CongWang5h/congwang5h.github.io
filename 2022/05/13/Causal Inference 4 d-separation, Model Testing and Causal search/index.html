d-separation

Definition 2.4.1 (d-separation) A path p is blocked by a set of nodes Z if and only if

  1. ppp contains a chain of nodes A→B→C or a fork A←B→C such that the middle node B is in Z (i.e., B is conditioned on), or
  2. p contains a collider A → B ← C such that the collision node B is not in Z, and no descendant of B is in Z.
V-structure Uncondition Condition
Fork unblock block
Chain unblock block
Collider block unblock

d-separation

“d” stands for directional

XXX and YYY d-separated iffiffiff XXX, YYY independent.
XXX and YYY d-seperated condition on ZZZ   ⟺  \iff⟺ XXX, YYY independnt | ZZZ

Unconditional d-separation

  • The collider blocks the path
  • A path is unblocked by fork, chain

Examples

  • In a fork
graph LR;
Z ---> X
Z ---> Y

XXX and YYY are d-connected.

  • In a chain
graph LR;
X ---> Z
Z ---> Y

XXX and YYY are d-connected.

  • In a collider
graph LR;
X ---> Z
Y ---> Z

XXX and YYY are d-separated.

More complex graphs

  • XXX and YYY are d-separated if every path between XXX and YYY is blocked, or d-separated.
  • XXX and YYY are d-connected, if there existd an unblock path.
graph
Z_1 ---> X
Z_1 ---> W
Z_2 ---> W
Z_2 ---> Y

There is only one path between XXX and YYY, but it is blocked by collider WWW. Therefore XXX and YYY are independent.

Conditional d-separation

  1. Conditioning on a non-collider (Fork, Chain) ZZZ blocks the path at ZZZ
graph conditional d-separation
X->Z->Y X⊥Y∣ZX\perp Y\mid ZX⊥Y∣Z
X<-Z->Y X⊥Y∣ZX\perp Y\mid ZX⊥Y∣Z
  1. Conditioning on a collider ZZZ or a descendent WWW of a collider ZZZ unblocks the path at ZZZ
graph conditional d-separation
X->Z<-Y X≡Y∣ZX \equiv Y\mid ZX≡Y∣Z
